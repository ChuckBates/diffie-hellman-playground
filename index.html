Diffie–Hellman Playground

Pick a private exponent, compute a public value, exchange it with a partner, and watch both sides derive the same shared secret.

This is a learning toy. Real DH uses vetted groups/curves, strong randomness, and authentication (certs) to prevent man-in-the-middle.

Ready
Current group: p=, g=

You

Choose your private exponent a, compute your public value A = g^a mod p, share A publicly.

Partner (instructions)

  1. Open this same page.
  2. Use the same p and g (use preset or your shared link).
  3. Pick a private exponent b.
  4. Compute B = g^b mod p.
  5. Publicly exchange A and B.
  6. Each computes:
    • You compute S = B^a mod p
    • They compute S = A^b mod p
  7. Say your S out loud. It should match.

Important caveat: This demo does not authenticate identities. A man-in-the-middle can establish separate secrets with each side if they can modify messages. TLS prevents that with certificates/signatures.

What’s happening under the hood?

A = g^a mod p
B = g^b mod p
You compute S = B^a = (g^b)^a = g^(ab) mod p
They compute S = A^b = (g^a)^b = g^(ab) mod p
